EXP 02

Electronic Piano using 555 Timer IC

555 Timer IC · Astable Mode · Push Buttons · Speaker

Aim
To design and build an Electronic Piano using the 555 Timer IC operating in astable multivibrator mode, where pressing different push buttons produces different musical notes (frequencies) through a speaker, simulating the keys of a piano.
Theory
The 555 Timer IC is one of the most popular ICs used in analog circuits. In Astable Mode, it works as a free-running oscillator generating a continuous square wave.

Frequency Formula:

    f = 1.44 / ((R1 + 2×R2) × C)

By connecting different resistors to different push buttons, each button changes the RC time constant — producing a different frequency and therefore a different musical note.

Musical Notes & Frequencies:

Do (C4) = 261 Hz  |  Re (D4) = 294 Hz  |  Mi (E4) = 329 Hz  |  Fa (F4) = 349 Hz
Sol (G4) = 392 Hz  |  La (A4) = 440 Hz  |  Si (B4) = 494 Hz  |  Do (C5) = 523 Hz

An 8Ω speaker connected to pin 3 converts the square wave into audible sound.

Procedure
  1. 1Place the 555 Timer IC on the breadboard using an 8-pin IC base.
  2. 2Connect Pin 1 (GND) to negative rail and Pin 8 (VCC) to positive rail of the 9V supply.
  3. 3Connect Pin 4 (Reset) to VCC and Pin 5 (Control) to GND via a 0.01µF capacitor.
  4. 4Connect Pin 2 (Trigger) and Pin 6 (Threshold) together.
  5. 5Connect a 10µF capacitor between Pin 2/6 and GND.
  6. 6Connect 8 different resistors in series with 8 push buttons between VCC and Pin 7 (Discharge).
  7. 7Connect an 8Ω speaker between Pin 3 (Output) and GND.
  8. 8Power the circuit with a 9V battery.
  9. 9Press each button one by one — each produces a different musical note.
  10. 10Verify notes match expected frequencies using a frequency counter or mobile app.

Complete Hardware Setup
555 Timer IC on a breadboard connected to 8 push buttons — each with a different resistor — and an 8Ω speaker at Pin 3 (Output). Powered by a 9V battery with capacitors at Pin 2/6 and Pin 5 as per astable mode configuration.
555 Timer IC 8 Push Buttons Breadboard 9V Battery
🎹 Push Button Keys
8 tactile push buttons act as piano keys — Do to Do₂. Each button is connected in series with a different resistor (5.5kΩ down to 2.7kΩ) between VCC and Pin 7 (Discharge). Pressing a button changes the RC time constant, altering the output frequency at Pin 3.
🔊 Speaker Output
An 8Ω speaker is connected between Pin 3 (Output) and GND. The 555 Timer generates a square wave at Pin 3 — frequency determined by the resistor of the pressed button. The speaker converts this into audible musical notes ranging from 261 Hz (Do/C4) to 523 Hz (Do₂/C5).
⚡ 555 IC Wiring
Pin 1 = GND, Pin 8 = VCC (9V), Pin 4 (Reset) tied to VCC, Pin 5 (Control) bypassed via 0.01µF capacitor to GND. Pins 2 and 6 are shorted together with a 10µF capacitor to GND — this forms the astable oscillator that produces the continuous square wave output.
